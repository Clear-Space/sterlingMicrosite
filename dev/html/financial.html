Our Financial Strength

The Sterling Hall School continues to enjoy a strong and stable financial position as a result of near-capacity enrolment and prudent financial management.  Careful control of administrative expenses helped the school achieve an excess of revenue over expenses of $91,305.  This was achieved without the usual contribution to revenue from Annual Giving as all fund raising this year has been dedicated to our Building Leaders campaign to fund the construction of the new wing.  Annual giving revenue combined with major gifts for the Building Leaders campaign are reflected in an increase in deferred capital contributions of $825,083 which will support the construction of the new wing.

  During the year, and as part of the long-term strategic plan for the school’s properties, which include the new wing, the school took the decision to demolish the buildings on the property at 130 Bentworth Avenue, adjacent to south-west boundary of the school.  The space at 130 Bentworth will be used initially to facilitate the construction and related logistics of the new wing.  As a result of the planned demolition the carrying value of the 130 Bentworth property was written down and a non-cash charge of $570,433 was taken during the year.

  Our financial strength and the ongoing support of the SHS community have allowed Sterling Hall to maintain its focus on providing the very best boys’ educational experience, and to continue to improve our programs and facilities, including the addition of the new wing.

The Sterling Hall School Foundation

The Sterling Hall School Foundation was established in 2005 as a public foundation to financially support the activities of the school and to provide financial security for its future. Donations received through the Foundation support a broad range of the school’s capital and operating requirements.
