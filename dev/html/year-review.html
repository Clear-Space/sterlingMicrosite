Our Year in Review

A Message from Rick

Principal

2013-2014 was a year of inspiration and aspiration, fuelled both by renowned visitors to the school and by our own remarkable community. With the support of our dedicated families, faculty, staff and students, the year culminated in a bold vision for Sterling Hall and a truly exciting direction for our future. In September, we were enthralled with the talent and joy of the Kenyan Boys Choir, whose international acclaim includes performing at President Barack Obamaâ€™s inauguration.

Thank you to all of our volunteers, in particular our Annual Giving Chairs Deena Ginsberg and John Cundari, New Parent Giving Chairs Kate Hilton and Rob Centa, Grade 8 Giving Chairs Jim and Lisa Dale, and Alumni Giving Chair Matthew Chong 2003. These volunteers are committed ambassadors and their devotion to raising participation levels and funds needs to be rec- ognized.

Many community members are continuing to support SHS with multi-year gifts to the Robinson Family Fund. The endowed fund is managed by the SHS Foundation and will offer financial assistance to future students as soon as September 2015, in perpetuity. We are grateful to the generous RFF contrib- utors whose gifts will enrich the fabric of SHS for generations to come. Our goal with the RFF is clear: we want to bring the best students to Sterling Hall, regardless of their means to pay.

In closing, the efforts and leadership of the Advancement team are to be commended. Jill Palmer, who joined Sterling Hall as Executive Director of Advancement in October 2013, brings passion, expertise and energy to her role. Along with Jill, the entire Advancement team is wholly committed to en- suring Sterling Hall remains an outstanding school.

Rick Parsons
Principal

It is truly remarkable and impressive that 100% of our faculty and staff supported SHS with a gift this year.
